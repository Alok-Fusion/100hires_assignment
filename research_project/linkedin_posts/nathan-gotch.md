## Summary

Nathan Gotch shares his predictions for the future of SEO in 2026, emphasizing that AI-powered search experiences will fundamentally change how users discover information. He predicts that traditional organic click-through rates will continue to decline as AI-generated answers become the primary entry point for many searches.

He also highlights the growing influence of user-generated content (UGC), YouTube, Reddit, and social platforms as important data sources for large language models. While traditional SEO best practices remain essential, Nathan argues that businesses must adopt a broader "Search Everywhere Optimization" strategy that spans multiple search engines, AI assistants, and content platforms.

---

## Key Insights

* Organic click-through rates will continue to decrease as AI search expands.
* AI-generated answers will become the default interface for many search experiences.
* User-generated content will play an increasingly important role in AI citations.
* Traditional technical SEO remains an essential foundation.
* YouTube and social platforms are becoming major discovery channels.
* Businesses should optimize for multiple AI platforms rather than Google alone.

---

## Why This Matters

Nathan's predictions highlight the shift from traditional search engine optimization to a multi-platform visibility strategy. As AI assistants increasingly influence information discovery, businesses must optimize their presence across websites, videos, social media, forums, and AI search engines instead of focusing solely on Google rankings.
