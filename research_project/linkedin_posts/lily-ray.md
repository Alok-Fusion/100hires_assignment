## Summary

Lily Ray discusses the growing use of comparison pages and "alternative" pages in SEO and Generative Engine Optimization (GEO). While these pages can be valuable when created authentically, she warns that large-scale AI-generated comparison pages are becoming a common spam pattern.

She explains that many companies are mass-producing comparison content without genuine product testing, often relying on competitor reviews or AI-generated claims instead of first-hand experience. As these tactics become widespread, search engines and AI systems are increasingly shifting toward objective third-party sources such as Reddit, YouTube, Trustpilot, and G2 rather than relying on self-promotional brand content.

Her central message is that sustainable AI visibility depends on authenticity, evidence, and user trust rather than aggressive content scaling.

---

## Key Insights

* AI-generated comparison pages are becoming an emerging spam pattern.
* Product comparisons should be based on genuine testing and real experience.
* AI search engines increasingly prefer independent third-party reviews.
* Large-scale AI-generated comparison content carries long-term algorithmic risk.
* Authenticity and transparency are becoming stronger ranking signals.
* Sustainable SEO requires experimentation without sacrificing credibility.

---

## Why This Matters

As AI-powered search evolves, search engines are becoming better at identifying manipulative content. Businesses should prioritize authentic, experience-based comparisons supported by evidence instead of producing large volumes of AI-generated comparison pages designed only to influence rankings.

## Original LinkedIn Post

```text
I think the next frontier of spam fighting from the search/AI companies is going to hit one of the most popular page types in SEO/GEO right now: comparison and alternative pages.

These pages can work when they're authentic and based on genuine experience, but once marketers scale them with AI and create hundreds of nearly identical pages, they become an SEO pattern.

Google and AI companies increasingly rely on objective third-party sources like Reddit, YouTube, Trustpilot, and G2 instead of self-promotional brand content.

As always: test, experiment, learn—but building comparison pages at scale without authentic experience is a long-term risk.
```
