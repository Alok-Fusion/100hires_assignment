## Summary

Rand Fishkin shares findings from SparkToro and Datos' "State of Search Q3 2025" report, highlighting how search behavior is changing in the AI era. Contrary to popular belief, traditional search engines continue to dominate despite rapid AI adoption. Instead of replacing search, AI tools are becoming an additional discovery channel.

The report also shows that zero-click searches continue to increase, meaning users often receive answers directly within search engines or AI assistants without visiting websites. Google Gemini and ChatGPT have emerged as the leading AI platforms, while several competing LLMs have experienced slower growth.

Another interesting observation is that European users appear to be adopting AI tools faster than users in the United States, demonstrating regional differences in AI adoption.

---

## Key Insights

* AI search is growing alongside traditional search rather than replacing it.
* Zero-click searches continue to reduce website traffic opportunities.
* Google Gemini and ChatGPT currently lead AI search adoption.
* AI visibility has become increasingly important for marketers.
* Regional AI adoption varies significantly across markets.
* Businesses should optimize for both traditional search and AI-powered discovery.

---

## Why This Matters

The findings reinforce that SEO is evolving rather than disappearing. Businesses must now optimize for traditional search engines while simultaneously improving their visibility within AI-powered search experiences. Success increasingly depends on brand recognition, authority, and discoverability across multiple platforms.
