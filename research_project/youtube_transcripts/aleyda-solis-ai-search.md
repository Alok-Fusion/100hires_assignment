# Aleyda Solís - YouTube Research

**Video Title:** AI Search, SEO & GEO: How Search is Evolving

**Speaker:** Aleyda Solís

**Video Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pqrwpXpMM6s

---

## Overview

This discussion focuses on how traditional SEO principles continue to apply in the era of AI-powered search while highlighting the additional considerations required for optimizing content for large language models and AI search platforms. Aleyda explains that AI search should be viewed as an evolution of search rather than an entirely new discipline.

---

## Main Topics

### 1. SEO Fundamentals Still Matter

Strong technical SEO remains essential. Websites must still be crawlable, indexable, and technically accessible.

### 2. AI Crawlers

Unlike Google, many AI crawlers cannot properly render JavaScript. Critical content should therefore remain accessible without relying entirely on client-side rendering.

### 3. AI Search as Another Search Channel

Rather than replacing SEO, AI search represents another platform where users discover information. Businesses should optimize across Google, ChatGPT, Perplexity, and other AI systems.

### 4. Brand Mentions Matter

AI systems increasingly value brand mentions, community discussions, citations, and digital reputation alongside traditional backlinks.

### 5. Topic-Based Optimization

Instead of optimizing individual prompts, organizations should build topical authority across customer journeys, products, and user intents.

### 6. AI Visibility Measurement

Organizations should monitor AI visibility by comparing brand mentions, sentiment, citations, and share of voice across AI platforms rather than relying solely on rankings.

---

## Key Takeaways

* SEO is evolving rather than disappearing.
* AI crawlers require additional technical optimization.
* Brand authority and community signals have become increasingly important.
* AI search should be treated as another search platform within an overall SEO strategy.
* Businesses should measure visibility at the topic level instead of focusing only on individual prompts.
* Helpful, brand-focused content is more valuable than generic high-volume articles.
